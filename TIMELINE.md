# A Walk Through Human History

Each milestone below is annotated with **how long ago it happened** and **where it falls
in the last minute of the cosmic year** (universe compressed to one calendar year,
where 1 second is about 437 years).

## Deep prelude

- **~300,000 ya: Anatomically modern humans** (*Homo sapiens*) appear in Africa.
  Cosmic calendar: Dec 31, 23:52.
- **~70,000 ya: Cognitive revolution.** Symbolic art, complex language, long-range trade.
- **~45,000 ya: Humans reach Europe and Australia.**
- **~15,000 ya: Humans reach the Americas.** For the first time, our species spans the globe.

## The last 20 seconds of the cosmic year

- **~12,000 ya: Agriculture** (Fertile Crescent, then independently in China,
  Mesoamerica, the Andes, New Guinea). Villages, surplus, population growth.
- **~7,000 ya: First cities** (Mesopotamia: Uruk, Eridu).
- **~5,500 ya: Writing** (cuneiform in Sumer, hieroglyphs in Egypt soon after).
  **Recorded history begins here.** Everything before this is archaeology.
- **~5,000 ya: Bronze Age.** The pyramids of Giza (~4,600 ya) are closer in time to
  us than to the invention of agriculture.
- **~3,200 ya: Bronze Age collapse.** The first "end of the world" we have records of.
- **~2,800 to 2,000 ya: The axial age.** Buddha, Confucius, the Hebrew prophets, Greek
  philosophy: most of the world's living ethical traditions appear within a few
  centuries of each other.
- **~2,000 ya: Roman Empire / Han dynasty** at their height, connected by the Silk Road.

## The last 4 seconds

- **~1,400 ya: Rise of Islam;** classical knowledge preserved and extended.
- **~600 ya: Printing press** (Gutenberg, ~1440). Information begins to compound.
- **~530 ya: The Columbian exchange.** The two halves of humanity, separated for
  15,000 years, reconnect, with world-changing and often catastrophic consequences.
- **~410 ya: Scientific revolution** (Galileo's telescope, 1609).

## The last second

- **~260 ya: Industrial Revolution.** Energy use, population, and knowledge go vertical.
  Almost everything you can see around you right now was invented after this point.
- **~150 ya: Electricity, germ theory, telegraph.**
- **80 ya: Nuclear weapons** (1945). For the first time, the candle can put itself out.
- **65 ya: First human in space** (Gagarin, 1961); **57 ya: first humans on the Moon** (1969).
- **~35 ya: The Internet goes public.**
- **Now.** More humans alive (~8 billion) than existed in total for most of our species'
  history. The only species we know of, anywhere, that keeps records at all.

## The point

Pick any two "ancient" events and the gaps are stranger than you think:

- Cleopatra lived closer to the Moon landing than to the building of the pyramids.
- Woolly mammoths were still alive while the pyramids were being built.
- Oxford University is older than the Aztec Empire.
- The T-Rex lived closer in time to *you* than to the Stegosaurus.

History feels long from inside it. From outside, it has barely started.
